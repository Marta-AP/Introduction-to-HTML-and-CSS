Aalborg

Aalborg is the capital of North Jutland, and in a league of its own. A pocket-size city, with its unique atmosphere and something for everyone. Museums, cultural monuments and historic buildings with world-class architecture and art in abundance.

Hjørring

Hjørring

Hjørring

Hjørring

Culture is on the rise in Hjørring, ready to give you new experiences. You cannot go there without being struck by the cultural environment. Museums, theatres, art and gorgeous sculptures are visible in the city, and they help create a very special atmosphere.

Grenen

Grenen

Grenen

Grenen

Where two oceans meet: Skagerrak and Kattegat. This is Denmark's northernmost point and a unique natural phenomenon, where the oceans class and the waves culminate with each other.

Frederikshavn

Frederikshavn

Frederikshavn

Frederikshavn

Known to most as a traffic hub, but Frederikshavn has a lot more to offer. An unusually high number of shops and restaurants, and even the only palm beach in Denmark.

Bangsbo Botaniske Have

Bangsbo Botaniske Have

Bangsbo Botaniske Have

This area offers some of the region's most magnificent nature —formed during the recent ice age with high hills and deep gorges. The Botanical Garden is the perfect example; it will simply seduce you by the sight and smell of the flowers.

Bulbjerg

Bulbjerg

Bulbjerg

Bulbjerg

The only cliff in Denmark, an area which is often referred to as the shoulder of Jutland. This is the only bird cliff on the Danish mainland, and as such, the only breeding place of the black-legged kittiwake. In addition, its foundation contain pieces of history, as well.

Aggersund

Aggersund

Aggersund

Aggersund

A village with evidence of settlement dates back to the stone age. Close by, we find Aggersborg, the largest of Denmark's former Viking ring fortress, and one of the largest archaelogical sites in Denmark, which consists of a circular rampart surrounded by a ditch.

Nibe

Nibe

Nibe

Nibe

The beautiful old town by the Limfjord, filled with cozy houses, intricate streets and a wonderful atmosphere at the harbour. Nibe's story dates back to the 13th century, although the importance of Nibe did not get recognized before the 15th century, thanks to the herring industry.

Shelters

Sleep outside in the Danish nature

Imagine a room with a view. Now, picture yourself in the view. If you could, this is the experience you are looking for. Sleep on the forest floor, in small primitive campsites —primitiv overnatningsplads—, large primitive campsites —lejrplads—, and free camping —fri teltning— in more than 275 woodlands all over Denmark.

Our journey took us to Kajholm. Then, to Rendborg Hede and Lild Klitplantage. And, finally, to Skørbæk Gadekær and its gorgeous sunrise by the lake.
